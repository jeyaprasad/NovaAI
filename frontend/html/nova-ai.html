The Challenge

Why satellite data stays dark

Earth Observation generates petabytes of imagery. Without AI, most of it goes unread — and disasters go undetected.

🔍
Manual Interpretation
Trained analysts spend hours manually reviewing satellite images to extract basic land-use patterns — a bottleneck that doesn't scale.
⏱️
Slow Disaster Analysis
When floods or wildfires strike, response teams need imagery-based intelligence in minutes. Current pipelines take days.
🧩
Fragmented EO Insights
Spectral bands, NDVI indices, and radar data live in separate tools — no single system converts them into unified, readable insights.
📡
Inaccessible for Non-Experts
Complex satellite data formats and GIS tooling lock out environmental agencies, planners, and first responders who need answers fast.
How NOVA AI Works

Real-World Applications

What NOVA AI can see

From emergency response to sustainable agriculture — one platform, many frontiers.

🌊
Flood Detection & Disaster Monitoring
Water body segmentation and change detection identify flood spread in near real-time, enabling faster emergency mobilization.
🌳
Deforestation & Environmental Tracking
NDVI-based vegetation analysis quantifies forest cover loss and tracks ecosystem health over months or decades.
🏙️
Urban Expansion Analysis
Temporal satellite comparisons reveal city growth patterns, guiding infrastructure planning and land-use policy.
🌾
Agriculture & Vegetation Monitoring
Crop health indices and seasonal vegetation maps support farmers and food security agencies with precision insights.
